What this means
Canada wants workers, not just residents
Targeting 64% skilled worker share by 2027–28 — the highest in decades. Immigrants are selected based on specific jobs waiting for them.
Closing the student visa backdoor
Some colleges issued visas with little academic value as a path to residency. IRCC cut approvals 35% in 2024 and now ties permits to accredited programs only.
Immigration as a hiring pipeline
IRCC works directly with energy, mining, and tech employers — visas only issued where a verified skills gap exists.

Economic Outlook
Can the strategy work? Labour force participation rate & GDP per capita — the two metrics that determine whether targeted immigration actually closes the gap
Outlook
Labour force participation rate (%)
GDP per capita — constant 2015 USD · IMF projection to 2029
LFP historical LFP projected GDP per capita historical GDP per capita IMF projected
Labour participation still falling
Down from 67.3% in 2008 to 64.2% today. RBC projects a further 2+ point drop by 2030 as the last boomers exit.
GDP per capita recovering slowly
GDP per capita shrank 1.4% in 2024. IMF projects recovery to ~$46.8k by 2029, but at 1.7% CAGR — more total GDP masked by more people.
Healthcare costs accelerating
Per-capita healthcare spending for 65+ is 4–5x higher than working-age adults. With 1 in 5 Canadians now over 65, total health spending is projected to rise 88% by 2040.
The targeted strategy is still early
The IRCC pivot began in 2024–25. If economic-class share reaches 64% and sector gaps are filled, the participation floor could stabilize by 2030.
