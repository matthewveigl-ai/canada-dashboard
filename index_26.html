What this means
Canada wants workers, not just residents
Targeting 64% skilled worker share by 2027–28 — the highest in decades. Immigrants are selected based on specific jobs waiting for them.
Closing the student visa backdoor
Some colleges issued visas with little academic value as a path to residency. IRCC cut approvals 35% in 2024 and now ties permits to accredited programs only.
Immigration as a hiring pipeline
IRCC works directly with energy, mining, and tech employers — visas only issued where a verified skills gap exists.

Can the strategy work? Labour force & economic outlook

Labour participation still falling
Down from 67.3% in 2008 to 64.2% today. RBC projects a further 2+ point drop by 2030 as the last boomers exit. Targeted immigration alone cannot offset this at current volumes.
GDP per capita is stagnating
GDP per capita shrank 1.4% in 2024 — the third consecutive year of per-person contraction. More total GDP masked by more people. Canada needs productivity gains, not just headcount.
The targeted strategy is still early
The IRCC pivot only began in earnest in 2024–25. If economic-class share reaches 64% and sector gaps in energy, AI, and healthcare are filled, the participation floor could stabilise by 2030.
